User Engagement &
Retention.

Overview

A video game publisher was losing ~6% of its live-service game's subscriber base each quarter despite regular major content updates. The goal: examine user behavior before and after the latest update and identify the driving force behind the dropping numbers.

Problem Statement

Subscriptions were in decline, but social listening was proving insufficient to identify the contributing factors. The business needed to measure player engagement and determine why the content updates were not slowing the churn rate.

Methodology

The project followed a standard analysis procedure with emphasis on interpretability (the product team needed to understand why subscriptions were dropping, not just that they were):

  • Extracted 4 months of game session data from the database via SQL
  • Used Python to clean up data quality issues, including missing values, incorrect data types, and inconsistent formatting
  • Engineered a metric to measure player engagement based on days logged in per week, game session length, and participation in social events
  • Added calculated fields to the dataset via Python to track user's engagement scores
  • Visualized the data in Tableau and compiled relevant views into a dashboard that told the data's story
