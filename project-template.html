Customer Churn
Prediction.

[ dashboard / chart screenshot ]

Overview

A SaaS company was losing ~8% of its subscriber base each quarter but had no systematic way to predict which customers were at risk before they cancelled. The goal: build a production-ready churn model that the retention team could act on weekly.

Problem Statement

Reactive cancellation surveys captured reasons after the fact, but provided no lead time for intervention. The business needed a proactive score — a probability of churn in the next 30 days — attached to every active account, refreshed weekly.

Methodology

The project followed a standard ML lifecycle with emphasis on interpretability (the retention team needed to understand why a customer was at risk, not just that they were):

  • Extracted 18 months of event-level usage data from the product database via SQL
  • Engineered 40+ features across engagement, billing history, support tickets, and account age
  • Trained a gradient-boosted classifier (XGBoost) with SMOTE oversampling to handle class imbalance
  • Evaluated models using precision-recall AUC; final model achieved 0.81 AUC on held-out test set
  • Used SHAP values to produce per-account explanations surfaced in the Tableau dashboard
[ SHAP feature importance chart ]

Key Findings

The three strongest predictors of churn were:

  • Declining weekly active usage in the 4 weeks prior (feature importance: 0.31)
  • No integration with any third-party tool in the account's history (0.18)
  • More than one support ticket marked "unresolved" in the trailing 60 days (0.14)

Accounts with all three risk factors had a 64% observed churn rate vs. the 8% baseline — providing a highly targetable cohort for the retention team.

Outcome & Impact

The model and accompanying Tableau dashboard were deployed in Q3 2023. In the first full quarter of operation, the retention team ran targeted outreach on the top 150 at-risk accounts per week. Measured results:

  • Quarterly churn rate dropped from 8.1% to 5.9% (−27%)
  • Estimated annualized revenue retained: ~$1.4M ARR
  • Retention team efficiency improved — 3× fewer manual account reviews per week

Tools & Technologies

Python (Pandas, Scikit-learn, XGBoost, SHAP, Matplotlib), PostgreSQL, dbt, Tableau, GitHub Actions for weekly model refresh.
